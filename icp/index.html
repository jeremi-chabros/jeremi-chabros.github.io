Cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) dynamics

Since my Student Selected Component last spring, I have been working with the Brain Physics Lab in the Division of Neurosurgery at the University of Cambridge. My current computational project with Dr. Peter Smielewski (Principal Investigator) and Dr. Alexis Joannides (Honorary Consultant Neurosurgeon) investigates optimising the mathematical model of cerebrospinal fluid dynamics in order to improve diagnostics of CSF disorders.

By analysing CSF infusion studies using a mathematical model of CSF dynamics, it is possible to estimate a number of diagnostic parameters: reference ICP (\(P_0\)), CSF formation rate (\(I_f\)), resistance to CSF outflow (\(R_{out}\)), and brain elastance coefficient (\(E\)). This is done by fitting an analytical solution to the non-linear differential equation describing the Marmarou model to measurement data, thus optimising values of \(R_{out}\), \(E\) and \(P_0\). However, these estimations may produce values that lie outside of physiologically permissible ranges e.g. due to the problem of local minima. In the current work, we propose an improvement to the gradient descent methods used in ICM+ by employing:

  • Hybrid nonlinear optimisation with penalties and transformation of objective variables
  • Bayesian optimisation framework.
